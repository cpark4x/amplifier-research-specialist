Subject: Thoughts on AI Document Processing Opportunity

Team,

I've been thinking about the document processing space and I believe there's a significant opportunity for us. The market is growing rapidly — everyone's talking about it and the big players haven't really nailed the solution yet.

From what I've seen, companies are spending a lot of time and money on manual document processing. It's one of those problems that seems like it should have been solved by now but hasn't. There are some competitors out there but their solutions are clunky and hard to integrate. I've heard from several potential customers that they're frustrated with what's available.

Our team has some relevant experience from the data extraction work we did last year. I think we could build something meaningfully better than what's on the market today. The technology is mature enough now that we could probably get a working prototype out relatively quickly.

The business case feels strong to me. Companies would pay for a good solution here — the ROI on automating document processing is substantial. I've talked to a few people in the industry and there's genuine interest.

I think we should seriously consider this. The window of opportunity won't stay open forever as the larger vendors will eventually catch up. We should discuss at next week's leadership meeting.

Key things I'm less sure about:
- Exact market size (it's big, but I haven't found reliable numbers)
- Whether to target enterprise or mid-market first
- Build vs. partner for the OCR component
- Pricing model (per-document vs. subscription)

Happy to dig deeper on any of these if the team thinks it's worth pursuing.

— Jamie
